# Human-centered Predictive Regenerative Control (HPRC)

## Project Objective

Develop a regenerative braking control method that reduces uncomfortable pitch motion during deceleration while maintaining stopping performance.

---

## Vehicle Parameters

Vehicle Mass : 1800 kg

Wheel Radius : 0.32 m

Initial Speed : 100 km/h

Maximum Regenerative Torque : 2500 Nm

Simulation Time : 10 s

---

## Conventional Braking

Stopping Time

7.40 s

Maximum Pitch Rate

12.50 deg/s

---

## RTSC

Stopping Time

7.80 s

Maximum Pitch Rate

4.66 deg/s

Pitch Rate Reduction

62.7 %

---

## Constraint Optimization

Constraint

Stopping Time ≤ 7.70 s

Optimal tau

0.30 s

Stopping Time

7.70 s

Maximum Pitch Rate

5.35 deg/s

Pitch Rate Reduction

57.2 %

---

## Conclusion

RTSC reduces pitch motion significantly.

Constraint optimization balances stopping performance and ride comfort.

Future work is to develop Adaptive HPRC using vehicle speed, brake pedal input and battery condition.

---

## HPRC v6 Final Candidate

### Control Parameters

tau_fast = 0.12 s

tau_comfort = 0.44 s

risk_gain = 0.16

### Simulation Result

Stopping Time

7.84 s

Maximum Pitch Rate

4.6206 deg/s

### Comparison

Conventional braking showed a maximum pitch rate of 12.50 deg/s.

RTSC showed a maximum pitch rate of 4.6568 deg/s.

HPRC v6 showed a maximum pitch rate of 4.6206 deg/s.

### Conclusion

HPRC v6 achieved slightly lower pitch rate than fixed RTSC while maintaining almost the same stopping performance.

This result suggests that estimating driver braking intention and pitch behavior can improve regenerative braking comfort.

---

## HPRC v7 Front-Rear Torque Distribution

HPRC v7 introduces adaptive front-rear regenerative torque distribution.

The baseline distribution is:

Front : 60 %

Rear : 40 %

When pitch rate increases, the controller reduces the front regenerative torque ratio and shifts more torque to the rear side.

This is based on the assumption that excessive front-side regenerative braking increases pitching motion, while rear-side regenerative braking can reduce pitch response.

Simulation result:

Stopping Time : 7.81 s

Maximum Pitch Angle : 2.30 deg

Maximum Pitch Rate : 1.9809 deg/s

Compared with fixed RTSC, HPRC v7 significantly reduced pitch rate while maintaining almost the same stopping time.
