# Physical Model for HPRC v8.1

## Longitudinal Load Transfer

During regenerative braking, vehicle deceleration causes longitudinal load transfer from the rear axle to the front axle.

The load transfer is estimated by:

ΔF = m a h / L

where:

- m : vehicle mass
- a : longitudinal deceleration
- h : center of gravity height
- L : wheelbase

In this project:

- m = 1800 kg
- h = 0.55 m
- L = 2.8 m

This load transfer is used to modify the front-rear regenerative torque distribution.

## Control Concept

When the estimated load transfer becomes large, excessive front regenerative braking may increase pitching motion.

Therefore, HPRC v8 reduces the front torque ratio and shifts part of the regenerative torque to the rear axle.

This approach aims to reduce pitch motion while maintaining regenerative braking performance.
